Teachings

I have experience as a teaching assistant for undergraduate introductory and intermediate microeconomics at ANU. Through this experience, I have learned to adapt my teaching to different learning styles while encouraging students to think independently and communicate economic ideas clearly.

Like my perception of good research, my ideal way of teaching is grounded in the integration of economic intuition, formal theory, and real-world examples. I aim to help students understand not only how models work, but why they matter. I emphasise clarity, intuition, and active engagement before technical detail.
